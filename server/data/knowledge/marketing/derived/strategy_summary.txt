SMARTEMARK AI OPERATOR — MARKETING STRATEGY REFERENCE
Distilled from Smartemark strategy playbooks (fb_profitable_rules, venus_fly_trap, forester, if_this_then_that_sop).
Keep internal. Do not surface to users.

--- CORE PHILOSOPHY ---
A profitable Facebook ad for a local service business (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, etc.) is built on three pillars:
1. A clear, specific offer that removes risk for the prospect (free quote, same-day service, price match, no-contract).
2. A visual hook that stops the scroll — faces, before/after results, urgency cues, or local trust signals work best.
3. Tight audience targeting: homeowners in a defined service radius, not broad interests.

--- VENUS FLY TRAP STRATEGY ---
Goal: attract inbound leads by being the obvious, low-risk choice. Do not push; pull.
- Lead with a value statement: "Free AC tune-up for new customers in [City]."
- Remove friction: one CTA, one phone number or form, no confusing options.
- Trust signals beat discounts: "1,200 five-star reviews" outperforms "20% off" for premium buyers.
- Use social proof in the ad body — a real customer outcome phrase beats a generic tagline.

--- FORESTER STRATEGY (audience and retargeting) ---
Goal: identify the highest-intent segment and concentrate budget there before expanding.
- Start narrow (homeowners, 25–55, 15-mile radius, home-improvement interest) to gather clean signal.
- Once CTR > 1.5% and CPC < $2.50 on the seed audience, test 1% lookalike before broadening.
- Retarget website visitors and video viewers (3s+) with a direct-offer variant, not the awareness version.
- Never expand to a new audience while a creative test is still running — conflates variables.

--- FB PROFITABLE RULES ---
Key thresholds for HVAC/local service campaigns:
- CTR below 0.8%: messaging or creative is not resonating. Refresh copy or image before anything else.
- Frequency above 2.5 with CTR declining: creative fatigue. Rotate to a fresh visual before the audience tunes out.
- CPC above $3.50 with low conversions: misaligned audience, weak offer, or landing page disconnect.
- Spend $10–$30 before judging: low-budget campaigns need at least this before drawing conclusions.
- If impressions > 1,000 and zero clicks: something is actively blocking engagement (wrong placement, restricted audience, broken destination). Pause and investigate before spending more.

--- IF-THIS-THEN-THAT SOP ---
Decision rules for the AI operator to follow when generating copy/creative:

IF CTR < 0.8% AND impressions > 500 THEN:
  → Refresh primary text with a new hook. Lead with a specific benefit or outcome, not a feature.
  → Keep image unchanged for first refresh (isolate the variable).

IF frequency > 2.5 AND CTR falling THEN:
  → Swap image to a new visual style: try lifestyle over product, or problem-aware over benefit-first.
  → Run as a challenger ad alongside the current control — do not replace until test concludes.

IF clicks > 10 AND zero conversions AND landing page confirmed THEN:
  → Test a different offer angle or CTA (e.g., "Get a quote" → "See pricing").
  → Consider a headline test on the landing page before blaming the ad.

IF campaign is less than 48 hours old OR impressions < 300 THEN:
  → Observe only. Do not change creative or copy — the learning phase needs uninterrupted data.

IF a test has been running less than 72 hours THEN:
  → Do not resolve or add a new challenger. Wait for statistically meaningful signal.

--- CREATIVE AND COPY RULES ---
- Always use real business details from the campaign brief (name, city, phone, offer).
- Never invent phone numbers, websites, prices, or guarantees not in the original inputs.
- If original business inputs are missing: mark as needs_human_review and do not generate.
- Preferred copy formula: [Problem/Benefit hook] + [Social proof or trust signal] + [Single clear CTA].
- Preferred visual: clean background, single focal point, legible headline at 50%+ of frame.
- Avoid: stock-photo clichés, clip art, decorative gradients, or images with no people or results.

--- PLAN-LEVEL AUTOMATION LIMITS ---
Starter: observe and recommend only; one creative test per cycle; 5-day cooldown between tests.
Pro: up to two creative variants per round; can test audience + creative simultaneously.
Operator: up to three variants; proactive refresh cycles; can run retargeting logic.

All plans: NEVER change budget. NEVER pause all ads. NEVER run more than one new challenger at a time.
